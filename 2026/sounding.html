How to Read This Chart

This is a sounding chart — named after the nautical practice of measuring depth at successive points along a voyage. Each line traces a single alliance through the phases of a match, showing how their score advantage or disadvantage evolved over the match.

The vertical axis is match phase, ordered from bottom to top: Initial → Auto → Transition → 1st Shift → 2nd Shift → Final. The horizontal axis is score differential — how many points ahead (positive) or behind (negative) that alliance was at the end of each phase. All matches start at zero.

Red lines trace the red alliance; blue lines trace the blue alliance. Because the differential is always mirrored (red's lead is blue's deficit), the two lines for any given match are reflections of each other across zero.

Filters: Use the Team box to isolate matches a specific team played in. Use the Match/Event box to narrow to a single event (e.g. 2026cmp) or a specific match key. You can also find just qualifying matches with qf The histogram below the sounding chart shows the distribution of alliance scores — drag to brush a score range and the sounding chart will update to show only matches that fall within it.
